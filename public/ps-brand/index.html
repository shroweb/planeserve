Foundation

Aircraft Program is a retained AOG (Aircraft-On-Ground) parts support route for private and business aircraft operators. Not a marketplace, not a travel product — a trusted operations desk for people who need hard-to-source parts handled fast when downtime is costing money.

Mission
Help owners and operators reduce downtime with a dependable route for AOG parts sourcing, aircraft profiles, document handling and operational updates.
Brand promise
When downtime matters, Aircraft Program gives operators a calm, capable team and system to move the request forward.
Personality
A competent ops controller — composed, technical, discreet, fast-moving. Premium without being luxury-heavy.
Feel
A private-aviation operations room rendered as software. Dense but calm; structure from borders, color used only as signal.

Positioning

Aircraft Program is
  • A retained AOG parts support lane
  • An operations desk you enrol with in advance
  • Calm, precise, technical and discreet
  • Quietly premium — trust over flash
Aircraft Program is not
  • A marketplace or instant-buy product
  • A travel or concierge-luxury brand
  • A generic SaaS dashboard
  • Loud, salesy or "revolutionary"

Imagery & Photography

Cinematic but credible aviation operations — hangars, maintenance, ground ops, business-jet detail. Warm-cool dusk grading, real depth. Photography always sits behind a dark ink overlay so type stays legible and the mood stays composed.

Hero treatment · ink overlay

Hard-to-source aircraft parts, handled fast.

Reference hero: business jet on the apron at dusk, tooling staged, technician at work. Warm-cool grade, real depth — credible, not staged.

Shoot & select

  • Hangars, aprons, maintenance and ground ops at dusk
  • Business-jet detail — gear, panels, tooling, livery
  • Warm-cool cinematic grade with genuine depth
  • Always pair with an ink overlay for legibility
  • Show competence and calm, not drama

Avoid

  • Holiday / travel or cabin-luxury hero shots
  • Vague dark silhouettes with no subject
  • Over-dramatic sunsets and lens flares
  • Fake customer-service stock poses
  • Decorative blobs, orbs or oversized SaaS gradients

Voice & Copywriting

Calm under pressure, precise and operational, clear rather than clever. It reads like a competent ops controller — never a marketer. Premium without being luxury-heavy; direct and human.

Person
Addresses the reader as you / your team / your aircraft. Aircraft Program is "the desk" or "the operations team" — never "we're revolutionising…".
Casing
Sentence case for headlines and buttons. UPPERCASE only for small mono eyebrows, tracked tight.
Sentences
Short, declarative, operational. One blue accent phrase inside a sans line for emphasis.
Numbers & IDs
Always monospace and tabular. Priority shown as a score plus reasons, never a vague label.

06

Do's & Don'ts

The non-negotiables. When in doubt, choose restraint — borders over shadows, neutral over color, signal over decoration.

Do

  • Let borders do the structure. 1px #DDE3E8 dividers, tight 4px radii, minimal shadow.
  • Reserve blue for action — CTAs, links and live AOG signal. Roughly 5% of any surface.
  • Set every number in mono, tabular: scores, IDs, tails, timers, phone numbers.
  • Use deep ink for dark surfaces. Sidebars, heroes and footers — composed, not harsh.
  • Keep one blue accent phrase per headline, maximum.
  • Write like an ops controller — calm, precise, what-happens-next.

Don't

  • Never use pure black (#000) or invent off-brand colors.
  • Don't flood with blue — overuse drains its meaning as a signal.
  • No emoji as icons. The only glyph is ⚠, reserved for critical admin banners.
  • No decorative gradients, blobs, orbs, parallax or bouncy animation.
  • Don't mix filled and line icons — Lucide, 1.5px stroke, throughout.
  • Don't write "revolutionary" marketing speak or luxury-travel language.
