01 / ESSENCE

What PlaneServe stands for.

A retained aircraft parts support route — not a public marketplace, not a travel site. Calm under pressure. Precise. Aviation-native.

Positioning

PlaneServe is the retained AOG parts route for private and business aircraft — keeping support profiles ready before downtime starts.

Mission
Reduce downtime for private and business aircraft by keeping aircraft context, sourcing data and documents ready for urgent parts work.
Promise
Submit the aircraft, fault, part and document details. PlaneServe gives the operations team the context to start sourcing quickly.
Voice
Calm. Specific. Operationally aware. Never breathless. Never glib about safety or availability.
Posture
Discreet by default. We assume the reader is busy and senior; we earn their time line by line.

02 / VISUAL DIRECTION

The room it walks into.

A quiet hangar desk, a live AOG queue, aircraft profile data and a document pack ready to check. Not a startup. Not a travel site. A standing arrangement with people who know what they are doing.

MOOD · 01INK
A support profile open during a night-shift AOG.
MOOD · 02LINEN
White cards, clean forms, and evidence on file.
MOOD · 03SIGNAL
One copper signal. That is the AOG action.

Feels like

A controlled operations desk with the right aircraft details already in view.

Editorial spacing on public pages. Dense, calm panels in product. Registrations and part numbers hold their own type system. Status is shown in language, not animation.

Never feels like

A consumer travel app, a generic procurement marketplace, or a SaaS dashboard on a pitch deck.

No neon gradients. No rounded mascots. No "Oops, something went wrong." No glassmorphism. No sky hero with a smiling pilot in a hi-vis vest holding a tablet.

Reference register
AVIATION

Pilot's Operating Handbook

Spec-sheet density, tabbed reference, plate-quality typography. The original operational document.

LOGISTICS

Maersk & Flexport consoles

Calm tables, status codes, dense data, low colour. Containers don't smile at you.

CURRENT APP

PlaneServe dashboard

Aircraft profiles, AOG priority, documents and admin actions should feel like one coherent operations surface.

PREMIUM B2B

Editorial operations tools

Functional minimalism. Dense but calm screens. Generous monospace. Adults talking to adults.

03 / LOGO

An operational mark, not an emblem.

The PlaneServe identity is a wordmark with a paired monogram. It reads first as a service line, second as a brand. No wings. No globes. No silhouettes of aircraft.

LOCKUP · PRIMARY · LIGHT CLEAR · 1× MONOGRAM
PlaneServe
CONCEPT

Two letters, one operation

The "P" is paper-white — what's on the record. The "S" is copper — what we did about it. Together: the request and the service.

WORDMARK

Geist 500, optical kerning

One word. Capital P and S only. Never "Plane Serve". Never all-caps in body text.

CLEAR SPACE

1× monogram on all sides

Reserve the height of the monogram around all edges of the lockup. No graphic or photograph crosses this band.

MINIMUM SIZE

Monogram 16px · Lockup 96px

Below 96px wordmark width, drop to the monogram alone. Never compress or substitute another typeface.

DON'T · 01

Set on photography of sky

The mark never floats over clouds, runways, or sunsets. Use solid Ink or Bone, or a flat Linen field.

DON'T · 02

Add a wing or tail device

No wings, swooshes, or aircraft silhouettes anywhere in the identity system.

DON'T · 03

Always solid. Never outlined, embossed, drop-shadowed, or set in a stroked variant.

08 / IMAGERY & ICONS

Metal, paper, and quiet rooms.

Photography should support the product promise: aircraft, hangars, support desks, documents and operational detail. Editorial, never aspirational. No stock pilots. No travel-site sunsets. Icons are 1.25px linework, never filled, with copper only for AOG signal states.

Photography register
IMG · 01HANGAR · 04:00
Aircraft in a clean hangar, with real maintenance context and visible registration cues.
IMG · 02APRON
Part, tool, label, fault indication or document detail — operational fragments.
IMG · 03PAPER
Tech log, release certificate, quote or trace document photographed flat on a desk.
DO

Documentary, off-axis, real

Use available light, clean hangars and mechanical detail. Crop into registration, parts, paperwork and admin surfaces. Photograph the operation, not generic aviation luxury.

DON'T

Sunsets, salutes, smiles

No staged crew portraits. No pilots in mirrored sunglasses. No wing-tip sunset hero. No marble lobbies. No luggage shots. No champagne flutes near aircraft.

Implementation notes
FONTS

Geist · JBM · Instrument Serif

Self-host where possible; Google Fonts as a fallback. Subset to Latin + numerals + a small punctuation set; ship 400/500/600 only.

DARK MODE

Mirror via [data-theme="dark"]

Re-bind ink/paper/border tokens at the root selector. Keep copper as #B98545 and use crimson for real blocked states.

MOTION

150ms ease · sparingly

Hover only on interactive elements. Never animate the AOG badge pip. No spring physics. No micro-interactions on data — operators read it, they don't watch it.
